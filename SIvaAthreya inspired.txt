Vertices live forever, and have some 'type' at any instant
Edges exist between each pair of vertices and are either ON or OFF at any instant

Initial conditions: nV vertices, nV(nV - 1) total edges

- each vertex is assigned a type out of *2* possible types: type0 with probability p0; type1 w/ probability 1-p0
# type0 vertices ~ B(nV, p0) and # type1 vertices ~ B(nV, 1-p0)

- probability of an edge between any pair of vertices being ON is pE
Hence # neighbours a given vertex ~ B(nV - 1 , pE)
and # total ON edges ~ B((nV - 1)*nV/2 , pE)
what about numbers of like/unlike neighbours? # type0 neighbours ~ B(nV - 1 , pE*p0)
Bear in mind that these distributions are solely relevant to the starting/seed state.


@ implementations
nV hardcoded manually
draw a 1D array V of nV numbers (corresponding to the nV vertices) from a uniform real distribution 
  np.random.uniform produces numbers in [0,1)
let's make all elements that are < p0 True and the rest False 
all vertices with value True shall take type0 and the rest take type1

draw a 2D array F of nV*nV numbers (corresponding to an edge matrix) uniformly from [0,1)
The goal is to make this a symmetric boolean matrix (with main diagonal zeros) such that each of the other elements has probability pE of being 1

let's make all elements that are < P True and the rest False. each element now has probability P of being True
now compute a new matrix E = F AND F.transpose
AND returns True only for inputs True and True
Main diagonal entries have probability P of being True because they are being ANDed with themselves - 0 AND 0 returns 0 and 1 AND 1 returns 1, which changes nothing.
All other entries are the AND of two independent inputs, and so have probability P^2 of being True

since we performed a commutative operation on a matrix and its transpose, the result is a symmetric matrix. we can use np.fill_diagonal on E to set the main diagonal to zeros (Falses) as per our requirement that self edges should never be on

we also required that the probability of each entry of E being 1 was pE -- if we choose P to be sqrt(pE), then P^2 = pE

Dynamics: vertices switch types, edges toggle ON/OFF

three principles to settle on:
• whether the change in state of the network at a given instant should depend solely on its state in the present moment, or on on past states/dynamics ("history")
[no history - can't apply history to 0th state as it did not exist then - 0th was randomly set]
• whether time should be continuous or discrete 
[discrete: "update steps"/'time steps'. no rates, delays, etc too much unwarranted complication]
• whether the types should be symmetric - that is, apart from the initial proportions of each in the network, should the different types behave differently in terms of dynamics coefficients, or should they be indistinguishable
[asymmetric - coefficient/probability table includes 'which type?' - does not sit well to have two types who are identical in all ways other than 'not being each other'. symmetry is a special case of asymmetry, better to make the program generic at the start]

Glossary
--------
counts:
  nV, of vertices
  nE, of edges
  Ni, of total neighbours
  Ui, of unlike neighbours

probabilities: 
  p0, of any vertex having type0 in the initial network condition
  pE, of any edge being ON in the initial network condition
  P, of any entry in the matrix F being True

arrays:
  V (nV,) of random floats in [0,1) and subsequently Bools corresponding to the types of the nV vertices
  F (nV, nV) of random floats in [0,1) and subsequently Bools
  E (nV, nV) and symmetric, of Bools corresponding to edges between each pair of vertices

functions:
  f(), gives the probability of a particular vertex switching type
